Wild Thoughts

An interactive web experience about the perception of different non-human species in Montreal

YEARS

2018-2020

MY ROLES

Web Design, Vanilla JS, User Interface Design, Animation

Animated squirrel and raccoon.

Cities are traditionally designed with the needs of one species in mind: humans. With the exception of a select few chosen species, most non-humans are shut out from human-dominated environments. Yet, some non-human species make it their homes regardless. These non-humans are called synanthropic species, meaning that they thrive in urban environments.

Animated coyote and house sparrow.

Taking inspiration from the questions asked by Fidino et al.'s 2018 study "Assessing online opinions of wildlife through social media", I developed a quiz that could help evaluate one's own attitude towards wildlife of Montreal. Using 5 different species, the quiz uses specific statements, sentences and words to classify the user's attitude according to the 9 Kellert attitudes, each of which are distinct attitudes toward wildlife.

The 9 Kellert attitudes are negativistic, doministic, utilitarian, aesthetic, scientistic, moralistic, humanistic, ecologistic and naturalistic.

I first created this quiz for a web design class in 2018, but was not happy with the design and the website structure. In summer 2020 I took it upon myself to redesign it to reflect my new knowledge of user interface design and animation. I am pretty pleased with the final result.
