Wild Thoughts Quiz

An interactive web experience about the perception of different non-human species in Montreal

YEARS

2018-2020

ROLES

Web Design, Vanilla JS, User Interface Design, Animation

Cities are traditionally designed with the needs of one species in mind: humans. With the exception of a select few chosen species, most non-humans are shut out from human-dominated environments.

Animated squirrel and raccoon.

Yet, some non-human species make it their homes regardless. These species are called synanthropic species, meaning creatures that thrive in urban environments.

Animated coyote and house sparrow.

Taking inspiration from the questions asked by Fidino et al.'s 2018 study "Assessing online opinions of wildlife through social media", I developed a quiz that could help evaluate one's own attitude towards wildlife of Montreal.

Using 5 different species, the quiz uses specific statements, sentences and words to classify the user's attitude according to the 9 Kellert attitudes, each of which are distinct attitudes toward wildlife.

A wordcloud I generated using the most popular words used by respondents on their ideal compassionate image.

The 9 Kellert attitudes are negativistic, doministic, utilitarian, aesthetic, scientistic, moralistic, humanistic, ecologistic and naturalistic.

In my first iteration of the home interface, I imagined the kit as a backpack sitting in a safe place, which the user could open up to remind them of their compassionate selves. However, as I continued to work on it, it became apparent that the interaction should be with the items inside the kit, and not the kit itself. With this new lead, I began developing templates for safe places in which the items could be displayed: ocean, forest, bedroom and sky. I named the app carekit and developed a logo and colour palette for the interface.
